# Invoice QR Code & Barcode — Official GST Standards

## Official Alternative to Barcode: QR Code

Per **CBIC Notification No. 14/2020-Central Tax** and **Circular No. 156/12/2021-GST**:

> A QR code is a quick response code. It is a **two-dimensional version of a barcode** and can be scanned from any mobile device.

**For GST invoices, the official standard is QR code, not 1D barcode.**

### B2B Invoices (e-Invoicing)

- **Source**: Invoice Registration Portal (IRP) — [einvoice6.gst.gov.in](https://einvoice6.gst.gov.in)
- **QR content**: IRN (Invoice Reference Number), HSN, invoice value, date, number, GSTIN of supplier & recipient
- **Applicability**: Turnover > ₹5 crore (w.e.f. 1 Aug 2023)

### B2C Invoices (Dynamic QR)

- **Source**: Self-generated by taxpayer
- **QR content** (per Circular 156/2021):
  - Total invoice value
  - GST breakup (CGST, SGST, IGST, CESS)
  - Invoice number & date
  - Payee's Bank A/C & IFSC (or UPI ID — linked to bank)
  - Supplier UPI ID
  - Supplier GSTIN
- **Applicability**: Turnover > ₹500 crore (B2C supplies)
- **Deemed compliance**: UPI QR for payment + cross-reference of payment on invoice

### Execora Implementation

- **UPI QR**: Generated on every invoice PDF when `upiVpa` is set (tenant settings)
- **Format**: `upi://pay?pa=...&pn=...&am=...&cu=INR` — scan-to-pay
- **IRP QR**: Stored in `invoice.qrCode` when e-invoicing is integrated (IRN from portal)
